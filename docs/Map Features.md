# Map Features

- Slippery Tiles
- Trap Tiles
- Unlockable Shortcut Tiles
- Event Tiles
- Teleport Tiles
- Rest / Save Tiles
- Doors
- Walls
- Hidden Path
- Switches
- Staircases up and down
- Obstacles
- Home Teleports

### Slippery Tile

When the player steps on this tile, they will continue moving every turn, and won't be able to change their direction until they hit an obstacle or a regular tile.
### Trap Tile

Trap Tiles have various effects that are triggered automatically when the player steps on them.  A trap tile can poison the player, hurt them, teleport them, slows them down. (We can add more later on)

### Unlockable Shortcut Tiles

Tiles that allow you to teleport through one point to another. They can only be unlocked from one direction, but once unlocked can be used from both sides.
### Event Tiles

Events are little mini text adventures that may hinder or reward the player in various ways. Every event should be unique.
### Teleport Tiles

These tiles teleport the player to a set location.
### Rest / Save Tiles

The player may rest to recover HP and/or create a quicksave.
### Doors

Doors allow the player to go from room to room. 
### Walls

Walls acts as the limit of each floor and rooms. Some enemies may be able to pass through them.
### Hidden Path

Hidden paths are ways that are hidden by illusory walls, they lead to different parts of a map.
### Switches

Switches are used to open doors, lift bridges, or trigger traps to open a way that might  be closed.
### Staircases Up and Down

Staircases are the exits and entrances of each floor.
### Obstacles

Obstacles are natural tiles that bar the player's progress and  may need specific items or magic to be taken out.
### Home Teleports

Every 5 floors of the labyrinth, there is a teleport tile that will allow the player to teleport out of the labyrinth and into the hub map.
